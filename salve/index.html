Common Questions

Salve FAQ

What's the difference between broad-spectrum and full-spectrum CBD?
Both contain multiple cannabinoids and terpenes from the hemp plant. The difference: broad-spectrum has had THC removed (so it's federally compliant at <0.3% THC, which our salve is). Full-spectrum keeps the THC in. We use broad-spectrum because the salve is intended for daily, anytime use without psychoactive effects.
Will this show up on a drug test?
Broad-spectrum CBD contains less than 0.3% THC by federal definition, and topical application generally doesn't enter the bloodstream the way ingestion does. That said, we can't guarantee individual test outcomes — they depend on the test, the threshold, and how much salve is used. If a customer is tested regularly for THC, they should consult their testing program's guidance.
How often can it be used?
As often as needed. There's no toxicity ceiling. Most people apply 1–3 times a day to a specific area. Some keep a tin at their desk for daily use, others save it for after workouts or before bed.
Is this regulated by the FDA?
The FDA has not evaluated CBD for specific health claims. We do not market this product to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease. We comply with FDA labeling requirements for topical hemp-derived CBD products. The salve is manufactured in a registered facility and tested by an independent third-party lab for every batch.
How long does a tin last?
Depends on usage. Lightly used on a small target area, the 2oz (1500mg) jar lasts 2–3 months. Heavy daily use across multiple areas, the 4oz (3000mg) is typically a 4–6 month supply for one person. The 0.5oz (375mg) is a 3–4 week trial size.
Why Texas beeswax specifically?
Texas beeswax has a higher melting point than most commercial beeswax, which gives the salve its specific texture — solid enough to scoop, soft enough to melt instantly on skin. We source it from a small Texas apiary that doesn't process the wax aggressively, which preserves more of the natural enzymes and antibacterial properties.
